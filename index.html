Project Summary

Project Title

Virtual Stress Relief Nature Park

Objective

To create a short, hands-on stress relief experience where users can relax through light, playful interaction instead of passive meditation. The project targets digital fatigue and burnout by offering a calm space with simple tasks that are easy to understand and satisfying to perform.

Target Audience

  • University and college students facing academic pressure
  • Software developers and remote workers with high screen time
  • People who find traditional meditation boring or hard to follow
  • New VR users who prefer simple, gentle interaction instead of complex games

Key Features

  • Apple picking interaction using VR-style hand controllers
  • Physics-based cube stacking that feels natural and calming
  • Nature-inspired environment with soft lighting and ambient sound
  • Pressure-free experience with no scores, timers, or competitive elements
  • Low learning curve suitable for 2–5 minute mental breaks

Technologies Used

  • Unity 3D
  • C# Scripts
  • XR Interaction Toolkit
  • Blender / 3D Assets
  • OBS Studio / Screen Capture
  • GitHub (Version Control)

Process, Challenges & Solutions

Design & Development Process

  • Research: Looked at existing relaxation and meditation apps to identify gaps
  • Ideation: Chose simple interactions (apple picking, cube stacking) as the core mechanic
  • Prototyping: Built an initial Unity scene with basic interactions and placeholder assets
  • Iteration: Improved lighting, physics, and environment layout based on feedback
  • Polish: Optimized performance, refined interactions, and prepared the final demo build

Key Challenges & How We Solved Them

  • Interaction Accuracy: Early versions made grabbing apples inconsistent.
    We adjusted collider sizes, interaction distances, and XR grab settings to make it feel more reliable.
  • Cube Physics Stability: Stacks were collapsing too easily.
    We tweaked rigidbody mass, drag, and surface placement to make stacking more predictable.
  • Performance Drops: Too many objects and dynamic lights affected frame rate.
    We reduced unnecessary objects, baked lighting where possible, and simplified some meshes.
  • User Comfort: Camera position and movement initially caused discomfort.
    We locked unnecessary rotations, refined player height, and kept the environment stable and grounded.

Future Improvements

  • Additional scenes such as rainy forest, snowy field, or riverside area
  • Optional gentle guidance for first-time users
  • Support for more platforms and input devices
